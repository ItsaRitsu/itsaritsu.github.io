The Concept

The world tour
they sold you.

"Be A Global Citizen" seduces visitors into an AI-powered world tour of eight countries — each one a postcard stereotype, each one introduced by a real corporate commercial that plays in the country's language while your AI transformation renders.

The AI is seamless. The backdrops are beautiful. The commercials are real. And none of it is the actual country.

A personalized NFC passport places you inside each fantasy. Eight stamps later, the piece asks: what were you actually sold? And where do you find the real thing?

The Extension

Then find
something real.

When you complete the global tour, a second passport opens: the Local Passport. Five categories of Grand Rapids businesses — clothing, gifts, wellness, food, books & records — each with their own NFC stamps to collect.

The world tour was commercial. Your neighborhood is real.

Choose any ten local stamps across all five categories to earn your Grand Rapids Local Citizen prize. Go at your own pace. Come back over the 17 days of ArtPrize.

Eight Country Stations

Tap the flag.
Watch the commercial.
Receive your stamp.

Each station is a physical NFC tag embedded in a country flag display. Tap with your phone. A real corporate commercial from that country plays in its original language with English subtitles — buying the 30–40 seconds PrismaLive needs to render your transformation. When it ends, your passport stamp arrives.

Passport Registration

Eight Country Stations

Tour eight stations at your own pace: Japan, India, France, Kenya, Saudi Arabia, Colombia, China, USA. Each marked by a country flag NFC tag.

30–40 sec · Real commercial · Randomly selected

Live Gallery Screen

Every transformation displays in real time on a large central screen. A constantly updating gallery of Grand Rapids residents placed inside the world — visible beyond the installation walls.

Congratulations,
Global Citizen.

You just toured eight countries. You were placed inside a cherry blossom festival, a Bollywood market, the Eiffel Tower at night. It was beautiful. It was seamless.

It was also a Toyota commercial. An L'Oréal commercial. A Samsung commercial. Each one a few seconds of beautiful imagery constructed to make you feel something about a place without knowing anything real about it.

The world is worth seeing — but find the people, not the postcard. Find authenticity, not advertising. And start right here, right now, in your own neighborhood.

The Local Passport is waiting. Twenty Grand Rapids businesses across five categories. Every one of them is the real thing.
